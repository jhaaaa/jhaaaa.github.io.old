About Haiku Mornings

👋 I’m J-Ha. That’s 90s-speak for Jennifer Hasegawa.

I’m a UX writer, technical writer, and information architect.

At a previous job, I learned to use git to maintain technical documentation. It was a love/hate relationship.

Now that my current job doesn’t involve git, I found myself missing it.

I don’t have any gitfu moves, but a lot of 🩸, 😅, and 😭 went into learning the few moves I know and I don’t want to lose them!

So, I created this site using GitHub Pages and Jekyll, and I use git to post a daily haiku.

✍️✍️✍️

Oh, I’m also a poet, photographer, and performance artist.

My poetry manuscript, La Chica’s Field Guide to Banzai Living, received the San Francisco Foundation’s Joseph Henry Jackson Literary Award in 2014 and will be published by Omnidawn in Spring 2020. 🎉💃🏻🤓

My work appears in The Adroit Journal, Bamboo Ridge, Tule Review, the San Francisco Bay Guardian, Transfer, and a forthcoming issue of Bennington Review.

You can learn more about me as a poet and photographer at J-Ha Land.
